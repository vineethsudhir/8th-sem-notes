  • What is IoT?
    • IoT is a technology that basically tries to give internet connectivity to devices previously not connected to the internet.
    • Allows the physical world to be smart and controlled through an intelligent network
    • Integrates the physical world and computers allowing improvements in efficiency, accuracy, automation, and enables advanced applications.

  • IoT and Digitization
    • Digitizationis the conversion of information into a digital format. It is focused on connecting “things” with its data and business result.
    • In the context of IoT, digitisation brings together things, data, and business process to make networked connections more relevant and valuable.
    • Smart objects and increased connectivity drive digitization, and thus many companies, countries, and governments are embracing this growing trend.
  • Impact of IoT
    Managing and monitoring smart objects using real-time connectivity enables data-driven decision making which results in the optimisation of systems and processes and delivers new services that save time while improving the overall quality of life.
    • Connected Roadways
      IoT is a necessary component for implementing a fully connected transportation infrastructure.
      • Provide better interaction with the transportation system through bidirectional data exchanges.
      • Provide important data to the riders.
      • Provide reliable communications and data from transportation-related sensors.

      Benefits of connected roadways

      • Reduced traffic jams and urban congestion.
      • Decreased casualties and fatalities.
      • Increased response time for emergency vehicles.
      • Reduced vehicle emissions.

      IoT applications in connected roadways

      • Intersection Movement Assist: Warns a driver (or a self-driving car) when it is not safe to enter an intersection due to a high probability of a collision.
      • Automated Vehicle tracking: A vehicle’s location is used for notification of arrival times, theft prevention, or highway assistance.
      • Cargo Management: Provides precise positioning of cargo as it is en route so that notification alerts can be sent to a dispatcher and routes can be optimized for congestion and weather.
      • Road Weather Communications: Use sensors and data from satellites to avoid dangerous conditions.

    • Connected Factories

      The main challenges facing manufacturing in a factory:

      • Accelerating new product and service introductions to meet customer and market opportunities.
      • Increasing plant production, quality, and up-time while decreasing cost.
      • Mitigating unplanned downtime.
      • Securing factories from cyber threats.
      • Decreasing high cabling and re-cabling costs.
      • Improving worker productivity and safety.
      A convergence of factory-based operational technologies and architectures with global IT networks is referred to as the connected factory.
      The IoT wave of Industry 4.0 takes manufacturing from a purely automated assembly line model of production to a model where the machines are intelligent and communicate with one another. IoT in manufacturing brings with it the opportunity for inserting intelligence into factories. This starts with creating smart objects, which involves embedding sensors, actuators, and controllers into just about everything related to production.
    • Smart Connected Buildings

In the past several decades, buildings have become increasingly complex, with systems overlaid one upon another, resulting in complex intersections of structural, mechanical, electrical, and IT components.
